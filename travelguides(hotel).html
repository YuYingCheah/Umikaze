Recommended Hotels

Hoshinoya Ryokan

1 Chome-9-1 Ōtemachi, Chiyoda City, Tokyo 100-0004, Japan

Take off your shoes, slip open your paper screen and sink into your five-star tatami futon. Tokyo's first five-star traditional ryokan inn, like many of the city's best hotels, is housed in a part of one of the city's skyscrapers; nonetheless, despite its central location, the ryokan maintains a quiet atmosphere.

The tranquil property boasts a stunning double-height genkan entrance, aromatic Japanese wood, and, of course, a massive indoor-outdoor hot spring spa fed from 1,500 metres beneath Tokyo. This facility is not family-friendly; children under the age of 12 are not permitted.

Andon Ryokan

2 Chome-34番地10号 Nihonzutsumi, Taito City, Tokyo 111-0021, Japan

This East-meets-West variation on the traditional ryokan, known as Tokyo's first designer hotel, will appeal to families in particular. The modern art-themed hotel is nestled in an unassuming residential street in the Taitō ward, and once inside, the bustle of the city disappears.

The modest rooms feature traditional tatami floors, chabudai dining tables and futons, as well as cable TV and DVD players to keep children entertained. Guests can also enjoy a stunning rooftop terrace and a spacious bathhouse inspired by modern artists from all around the world.
