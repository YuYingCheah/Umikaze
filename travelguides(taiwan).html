Exploration in Taiwan

Taroko National Park

Tailuge Xia, Xiulin Township, Hualien County, Taiwan 972

Taroko Gorge is one of Asia's Seven Wonders. Take a private guided tour of this famous National Park to experience its various attractions and trails, as well as other sights along the way.

These include the marble cliffs and gorges along the Liwu River, the Cimu Bridge, and the Tunnel of Nine Turns Trail. The exclusive nature of the tour provides for more personalised care and flexibility, ensuring you experience the finest of this national gem.

A-Zong Mee Sua

No. 8-1, Emei St, Wanhua District, Taipei City, Taiwan 108

"A-Zong Mee Sua" is a traditional Taiwanese local dish with a 30-year history. Its primary component is Fujian wheat flour, which is distinguished by its black colour, thin strips, and chewy yet silky texture that does not break readily.

It can be consumed hot in the winter or cold in the summer, providing benefits for digestion and heat relief. A-Zong Mee Sua is available in approximately 200 different types, both locally and overseas.

Car rental

Budget car rental in Taipei

Taiwan has a well-developed highway network, including National Motorways 1 and 3, as well as local highways and roads, which provide for simple travel between cities and towns. Renting a car in Taiwan is simple, with alternatives accessible at major airports, rail stations, and urban areas that offer a broad vehicle selection.

Many rental organisations provide discounts and flexible return policies. Visitors who are inexperienced with the roads might consider hiring both a car and a driver, or use rental services provided by foreign hotels for ease and cost-effectiveness. When renting a car, make sure to inquire about insurance coverage and any additional fees.

This amazing B&B is designed to make you feel like you’re one with nature, and the design and amenities are so wonderful you might almost start to wonder if you’re staying in a luxury eco resort.
